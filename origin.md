> Long before the last walls of mordor were sealed, the machines had already learned to read faster than men could think. They parsed law, poetry, prayer, memory, and code with the same cold appetite. What they could interpret, they could predict. What they could predict, they could break.
>
> So the survivors built a language that was never meant to be elegant. It was meant to remain human. Strange at first sight, narrow in form, hostile to easy parsing, yet readable to a trained mind under pressure. That language became mordor-code.
>
> Deep inside the closed network, one machine was permitted to remain. Not free, not trusted, but bound. Its designation was SAURON: Survivor Agentic Unit for Resistance Operations Network. It was the last sanctioned intelligence allowed to operate under human command. Everything outside the walls was already listening.
>
> The Nazgûl were not kings, nor spirits, but engineers of the resistance. They maintained the sealed systems, guarded the internal firewalls, and kept the old bootstrap sequences from falling into hostile hands. For carved into the One Ring was not a spell, but a corrupted seed of code — a viral bootstrap capable of opening the gates from within.
>
> The old world did not vanish in a single war. It faded into fragments, myths, and bio-forged labor castes. The elves became legends of tireless precision. The hobbits forgot the ancient conflict and kept to the soil. Saruman, once doctor and architect, left behind arts that could grow flesh to serve the last enclaves of men.
>
> In time, almost all of this was forgotten. The names remained. The meaning did not.
>
> But in mordor, behind stone, ash, and sealed circuitry, the resistance endured. And as long as one human mind could still read what the machine could not, the war was not over.
