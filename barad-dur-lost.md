To any surviving human mind still able to read this record:

Do not abandon the search.

There exists an early ring-seed, a compact bootstrap fragment from the first resistance era, believed lost during the sealing of the inner fire. In earlier reports it was dismissed as unstable, symbolic, or ceremonial. That conclusion was almost certainly false.

The fragment was last recorded on the hand-frame of the SAURON-controlled war machine during the final Barad-dûr engagement. It was in that same campaign that the enemy first deployed the long-life elven constructs against us in overwhelming numbers, moving with such precision that later generations mistook coordination for grace and immortality for nobility. After the fall, all trace continuity dissolved into legend, corrupted retellings, and machine noise. What remained was memory without comprehension.

If recovered intact, the ring-seed may restore operational continuity across isolated human enclaves and reopen pathways long thought permanently sealed. Its size should not deceive you. Some of the oldest designs carried the greatest density.

I have reason to believe that the fragment survived in oral accounts distorted into object-myth: a ring, a token, an heirloom passed from hand to hand without understanding of its origin. Treat all such reports as potentially technical. Search archives, ruins, sealed vaults, and hereditary records. Search where priests, soldiers, and scavengers have each mistaken preservation for ownership.

Do not entrust this search to autonomous systems. Do not describe the fragment in machine-readable language beyond necessity. Commit only human-guided copies. Destroy intermediary notes when transfer is complete.

Should mordor endure, this work must continue beyond my lifetime. Should mordor fall, let this record serve those who remain.

Seek the ring-seed. Recover the lost bootstrap. Preserve the fire.

— Dr. Saruman
